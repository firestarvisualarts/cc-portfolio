Experimental Clock

Description

This week's assignment was to create an experimental clock, using if/else statements.

Design Process

For the first part of this assignment, I was supposed to sketch out 3 clock types and choose one of those for the final one. However, I already knew what kind of clock I wanted to do, so I just went for it and started coding. I decided that, instead of my clock counting upwards, I wanted it to count downwards, like a timer. I have it set to count down until midnight.

Reflection

This one was interesting. At first, I didn't know how to get it to count down. I worked on it with a classmate during lab this past Friday, and we got it to work. However, when my any part of my clock gets into the single digits, it looks a little odd since a 0 isn't added in front of it. I tried to employ the if statement of the if/else statements, to no avail. You can even see it in my code. I don't actually know where I went wrong. This past Friday, my classmate tried it on her computer, and it worked, but for some reason, it doesn't work for me.

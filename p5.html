Optical Illusion

Description

Our task was to create an optical illusion using for loops, nested for loops, and if/else statements. We were supposed to take a pattern from our culture, and animate it in code.

Design Process

Once again, I didn't actual sketch anything out beforehand. I had an idea and ran with it. My idea was to do a Pride Pokeball (from Pokemon) pattern. So, I had a classmate of mine help me figure out how to get the the two tone of the regular Pokeball (red and white). After, I decided what I wanted to do with the pattern. I decided to have each Pokeball spin. I also received help from my professor. They helped me with the math and syntax of this assignment. Finally, I used an if/else statement to have the Pokeballs randomly change color.

Reflection

Whenever I get an assignment that asks me to take from my "culture", I always trip up on it. I was adopted from China at 9 months old, and raised in the United States by Caucasian parents. So, I don't really know what my culture is since I don't really identify with the Chinese culture, but I don't identify with American culture since I don't think there is one. The U.S. is a melting pot, so everyone brings their own cultures with them.

I spoke to a different classmate about this, and they said that I can create my own kind of culture. One that reflects all my experiences and values. It's still a work in progress, but I will get there eventually.

As far as this project, My original design was to have the Pokeball change color on mousePressed, but then I realized that I needed to incorporate an if/else statement. At first, I had trouble thinking where I could use one. I finally decided to use mouseIsPressed to create an experience similar to a disco ball.
